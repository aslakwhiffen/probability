
To find the distribution of the random variable $ X$, we can split the random variable $ (X,Y,Z)$
into $ (X, (Y,Z))$ so then applying theorem 14.13, we get that $ X \sim \mathcal N(\mu_{X} ,
\Sigma_{XX} )$ and
$ (Y,Z) \sim \mathcal N(\mu_{(Y,Z)}, \Sigma_{(Y,Z)(Y,Z)} $. Thus $ \mu_{X} = 1$ and $ \Sigma_{XX} =
2$.
