
Let's split the random variable $ (X,Y,Z)$ in a different way $ ((X,Y),Z)$. Thus, applying Theorem
14.13 again, we see that $ (X,Y) \sim \mathcal N(\mu_{(X,Y)} , \Sigma_{(X,Y)(X,Y)} $ which is
\[
    \Sigma_{(X,Y)(X,Y)} = \begin{pmatrix}
	\Sigma_{XX} & \Sigma_{XY} \\
	\Sigma_{YX} & \Sigma_{YY} 
    \end{pmatrix}
.\] 
Theorem 14.14 tells us that $ \Sigma_{XY} = 0$ if and only if $ X$ and $ Y$ are independent. So
since $ \Sigma_{XY} = 1 \neq 0$ we see that $ X$ and $ Y$ are not independent.
