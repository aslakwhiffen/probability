
Theorem 14.15 gives us the distribution of $ Y$ given the observation $ X = x$, where $ (X,Y) \sim
\mathcal N(\mu, \Sigma)$. The random variable $ Y$ is a normal distribution and it turns out that
its conditional distribution with the condition $ X = x$ is still a normal distribution which
follows
\[
    Y|X \sim \mathcal N(\mu_{Y} + \Sigma_{YX} \Sigma_{XX}^{-1}(x-\mu_{X} ), \Sigma_{YY} -\Sigma_{YX}
    \Sigma_{XX}^{-1} \Sigma_{XY} )
.\] 
We get that 
\[
    \Sigma = \begin{pmatrix}
	\Sigma_{XX} & \Sigma_{XY} \\
         \Sigma_{YX}& \Sigma_{YY} 
    \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix}
	2 & 1 \\
	1 & 3
    \end{pmatrix} 
\] 
and $ (\mu_{X}, \mu_{Y})^{T} = (1,2)^{T}  $ . Plugging in the values we get that 

\[
    Y|X \sim \mathcal N(0.5x + 1.5, 2.5)
\] 
and so the probability density function is then 

\[
    f_{Y|X} (y|x) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi} \sigma} e^{- \frac{1}{2} \left( \frac{y-\mu}{\sigma} \right)^2}  
\]  
where $ \mu$ and $ \sigma$ are now updated to $ \mu = 0.5x + 1.5$ and $ \sigma = 2.5$.
