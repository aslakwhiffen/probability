
We observe that we have an affine transformation on a random variable $ (X,Y)$ with a standard
normal distribution

\[
    \begin{bmatrix}
    U \\
    V
    \end{bmatrix} = A \begin{bmatrix}
    X \\
    Y	
    \end{bmatrix} + 0
\] 
meaning that according to Definition 14.5 

\[
    \begin{bmatrix}
    U \\
    V
    \end{bmatrix} \sim \mathcal N(\mu, \Sigma)
\] 
where $ \Sigma = A A^{T} $ and $ \mu = 0$. Theorem 14.14 provides a sufficient and necessary
criteria for $ X$ and $ Y$ to be independent, that is $ \Sigma_{UV} = 0$ if and only if $U $ and $
V$ are independent. Let's calculate the covariance matrix $ \Sigma$
\[
    \Sigma = A A^{T} = \begin{pmatrix}
	\cos \theta & - \sin\theta \\
	\sin \theta & \cos \theta
    \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix}
	\cos \theta & \sin \theta\\
	-\sin\theta & \cos \theta
    \end{pmatrix} = \mbox{Id}_{2} 
\] 
so we see that $ \Sigma_{UV} = 0$ and $ U$ and $ V$ are independent.
