
By Theorem 14.7 (Statistics of the normal distribution and the moment generating function), we have
that the covariance matrix $ \mathrm{Cov}(X,Y)$

\[
    \mathrm{Cov}(X,Y) = \begin{bmatrix}
	\mathrm{Var}(X) & \mathrm{Cov}(X,Y)\\
        \mathrm{Cov}(Y,X) & \mathrm{Var}(Y)
    \end{bmatrix} = \Sigma = \frac{1}{3}  \begin{bmatrix}
	2 & -1 \\
	-1 & 2
    \end{bmatrix}
.\]
In the previous part we showed that the expectation value of $ (X,Y)$ is the zero vector meaning
that the expectation values $ \E(X)$ and $ \E (Y)$ are equal to zero. This means that 

\[
    -\frac{1}{3} = \mathrm{Cov}(X,Y) = \E(XY) - \E(X) \E(Y) = \E(XY) 
\]
and so $ \E(XY) = -1/3$.
