
Before calculating the conditional expectation $ \E(Y |X = x)$, we need to find out what $ f_{Y|X}
(x,y)$ is. 
For each $ x \in [0,1]$, we have that $ f_{X} (x) > 0$ so $ f_{Y|X} (x,y)$ is defined for these $
x$. Let's show this and  find $ f_{X} $ to find out what $ f_{Y|X} (x,y)$ is.
\begin{align*}
    f_{X} (x) &= \int_{\R} f_{(X,Y)} (x,y) dy \\
	      &= \int_{\R} \left[ x^2 y + \frac{1}{12} y^{3}  \right] 1_{0 \leq y \leq 2} dy\\
	      &= x^2 \int_{0}^{2} ydy + \frac{1}{12} \int_{0}^{2} y^{3} dy\\
	      &= 2x^2 + \frac{1}{3}
\end{align*}
so we see that indeed for $ x \in [0,1]$ we have that $ f_{X} (x) > 0$.
Thus, for $ x \in [0,1]	$, we have that

\[
    f_{Y|X} (x,y) = \frac{f_{X,Y} (x,y)}{f_{X} (x)} = \frac{x^2y + 1/12 y^{3} }{2x^2 + 1/3}  1_{0 \leq y \leq 2} 
.\] 
Let's calculate $ \E(Y|X = x)$

\begin{align*}
    \E(Y|X = x) &= \int_{\R} y f_{Y|X} (y|x) dy\\
		&= \int_{\R} y \frac{x^2 y + 1/12 y^{3} }{2x^2 + 1/3} 1_{0 \leq y \leq 2} dy\\
		&= \int_{0}^{2} y \frac{x^2 y + 1/12 y^{3} }{2x^2 + 1/3} dy\\
		&= \frac{1}{2x^2 + 1/3} \left[ x^2 \int_{0}^{2} y^2 dy + \frac{1}{12} \int_{0}^{2}
		y^{3} dy  \right] \\
		&= \frac{1}{2x^2 + 1/3} \left[ x^2 \frac{y^{3} }{3}\left. \right]_{0}^{2} +
		\frac{1}{12} \cdot \frac{y^{4} }{4}\left. \right]_{0}^{2}  \right] \\
		&= \frac{1}{2x^2 + 1/3} \left[ x^2 \cdot \frac{8}{3} + \frac{1}{3}  \right] \\
		&= \frac{8x^2 + 1 }{6x^2 +1} 
\end{align*}
