
$ \Sigma_1$ is not symmetric so it cannot be a covariance matrix. $ \Sigma_2$ is symmetric and
satisfies $ x^{T} \Sigma_2 x \geq 0 $ since 
\[
    (v_1 \, v_2) \begin{bmatrix}
	1 & -1 \\
	-1 & 1
    \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}
    v_1\\
    v_2
    \end{bmatrix} = (v_1 \, v_2) \begin{bmatrix}
    v_1 - v_2\\
    -v_1 + v_2	    
    \end{bmatrix} = (v_1 - v_2)^2 \geq 0
.\] 
$ \Sigma_3$, although symmetric, is not positive semi
definite since 

\[
    (v_1 \, v_2) \begin{bmatrix}
	1 & 2 \\
	2 & 1
    \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}
    v_1 \\
    v_2	    
    \end{bmatrix} = (v_1 + v_2)^2 + 2v_1 v_2
\] 
and letting $ v_1 = 0$ and $ v_2 = -1$ get that the term on the right is equal to $ -1$, and
therefore $ \Sigma_3$ is not positive semi definite.
