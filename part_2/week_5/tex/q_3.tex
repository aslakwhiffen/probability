
The main idea behind the formula is the towering property of the conditional expectation value
(Theorem 13.14). Let's prove the formula from right to left, that is expanding the right hand side
of the formula and showing that it is equal to the left hand side.
The first term on the right hand side (RHS) is 

\begin{align*}
    \E \left[ \mathrm{Var}(g(X,Y) | X) \right]  &= \E  \left[  \E(g(X,Y)^2|X) - (E(g(X,Y)|X))^2 \right] \\
					&= \E( \E (g(X,Y)^2|X))- \E( \E(g(X,Y))|X)^2\\
					&= \E(g(X,Y)^2) - \E (\E(g(X,Y)|X))^2\\
\end{align*}
where in the last line we used the towering property, that is integrating over the whole domain of
the random variable $ X$ eliminates the condition.
The second term on the RHS is

\begin{align*}
    \mathrm{Var}( \E(g(X,Y)|X)) &= \E(\E(g(X,Y)|X))^2 - (\E(\E(g(X,Y)|X)))^2\\
			&= \E(\E(g(X,Y)|X))^2 - (\E (g(X,Y)))^2.
\end{align*}
Adding up these two terms on the RHS we get 

\begin{align*}
    \E \left[ \mathrm{Var}(g(X,Y) | X) \right] + \mathrm{Var}( \E(g(X,Y)|X)) &= \E(g(X,Y)^2) - (\E (g(X,Y)))^2\\
							     &= \mathrm{Var}(g(X,Y))
\end{align*}
