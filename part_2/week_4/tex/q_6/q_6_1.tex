
From the previous exercise (5) we showed that the marginal distribution $ f_{Y} (y)$ is

\[
    f_{Y} (y) = \int_{\R}  f_{(X,Y)} (x,y) dx = 2e^{-y} (1- e^{-y} ) 1_{0 < y}   
.\] 

By definition 13.2, the conditional probability 

\[
    f_{X|Y} (x|y) = \frac{f_{(X,Y)} (x,y)}{f_{Y} (y)} 
\] 
whenever $ f_{Y} (y) > 0$. Dividing we get the following

\begin{align*}
    f_{X|Y} (x|y) &= \frac{2 e^{-x} e^{-y} 1_{0 \leq x \leq y} }{2 e^{-y} (1-e^{-y} )1_{0 < y}  } \\
		  &= \frac{e^{-x} e^{y} }{e^{y} -1} 1_{0 \leq x \leq y} 1_{0 < y}  
\end{align*}
