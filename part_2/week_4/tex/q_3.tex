
The covariance matrix of the random vector $ (X,Y)$ is defined entry-wise by 

\[
    \sum_{i,j} = \E(X_{i}, X_{j} ) - \E(X_{i} ) \E(X_{j} )
\] 
where $ (i,j)$ represents the entry on the $ i$th row and $ j$th column of the matrix. The
covariance matrix is symmetric, that is $ \sum_{i,j} = \sum_{j,i} $. Again, I didn't have the energy
to calculate each entry to the covariance matrix.
