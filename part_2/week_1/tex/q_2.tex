
Consider the probability 

\begin{align*}
    \P( |X+Y| \geq a) 
\end{align*}

Since the set $ \{ \omega: |X+Y| \geq a \} $ is equal to the set $ \{ \omega: |X| + |Y| \geq |X+Y|
\geq a \} $, we see that

\begin{align*}
    \P( |X+Y| \geq a) &= \P( |X| + |Y| \geq a) \\
		      &< \frac{ \E(|X| + |Y|)}{a} \, \mbox{Markov's inequality}  \\
		      &= \frac{\E |X|}{a} + \frac{\E |Y|}{a}\\ 
\end{align*}
Let's find out what the expectation $ \E |X| $ of $ |X|$ is

\begin{align*}
    \E |X| &= \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} |x| f_{X} (x) dx\\
           &= \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \frac{ |x| e^{-x^2/2} }{\sqrt{2\pi}} dx\\
	   &= 2 \int_{0}^{\infty} x \frac{e^{-x^2/2} }{\sqrt{2\pi}} dx\\
	   &= 2 \int_{0}^{\infty} - \frac{d}{dx} (e^{-x^2/2})dx \\
	   &= - \frac{2}{\sqrt{2\pi}} \left[ e^{-x^2/2}  \right]_{0}^{\infty} \\
	   &= \sqrt{ \frac{2}{\pi}  }
\end{align*}

Now for $ |Y|$

\begin{align*}
    \E |Y| &= \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} |y| f_{Y}(y) dy\\ 
\end{align*}

Since $ Y$ is exponentially distributed with parameter $ \lambda = 1$, we have that $ f_{Y} (y) =
e^{-y} $ for $ y \geq 0$ and $ f_{Y} (y) = 0$ for $ y < 0$. Thus

\begin{align*}
    \E |Y| &= \int_{0}^{\infty} y f_{Y} (y) dy\\
	   &= 1 \, \mbox{when the parameter} \, \lambda =1 
\end{align*}

All in all,


\begin{align*}
    \P(|X + Y|) \leq \frac{\sqrt{ \frac{2}{\pi}} +1}{a} 
\end{align*}
