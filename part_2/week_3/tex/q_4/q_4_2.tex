
Following Theorem 12.11, we assume for contradiction that $ X_1$ and $ X_2$ are independent. Then

\[
    f_{X_1, X_2} (x_1,x_2) = \Pi_{j=1}^{n} f_{X_{j} } (x_{j} ) = f_{X_1} (x_1) f_{X_2} (x_2) 
.\] 
Assume that $ (x_1,x_2) \in [0,1] \times [0,2]$. Then 

\begin{align*}
    f_{X_{1} , X_2} (x_1,x_2) &= f_{X_1} (x_1) f_{X_2} (x_2) 
			      &=(2x^2 + 1/3)(y/12(4 + y^2)\\
			      &= \frac{y}{12} (8x^2 + \frac{4}{3} + 2x^2 y^2 + \frac{y^2}{3} ) \\
			      &\neq  x^2 y + \frac{1}{12} y^{3} = f_{X_1}(x_1) f_{X_2} (x_2)  
\end{align*}
Therefore $ X_1$ and $ X_2$ are not independent.
