
The set $ \Omega $ is neat enough and the two conditions for the function $ f$ from Theorem 12.2
hold, namely $ f(x) \geq 0$ for all $ x \in \Omega$ and $ \int_{\Omega}^{}f(x) dx = 1 $:

\begin{align*}
    f(x,y) = x^2y + \frac{1}{12} y^{3} \geq 0
\end{align*}
since $ x, y \geq 0 $ and 

\begin{align*}
    \int_{\Omega}^{} f(x) dx &= \int_{[0,1] \times [0,2]}^{} x^2y + \frac{1}{12} y^{3} dx dy\\
			     &= \int_{[0,1]}^{} \int_{[0,2]}^{} x^2y + \frac{1}{12} y^{3} dy dx \,
			     \mbox{using definition 11.4} \\
			     &= \int_{[0,1]}^{} x^2[y^2/2]_{0}^{2} + \frac{1}{12} [y^{4}/4]_{0}^{2}
			     dx \\
			     &= \frac{2}{3} + \frac{1}{3} = 1 
\end{align*}.
