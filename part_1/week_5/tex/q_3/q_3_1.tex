
By Definition 6.10, two random variables (defined on the same domain $ \Omega$) are independent if

\[
    \P(X_1 \in A_1, X_2 \in A_2) = \P(X_1\in A_1) \P(X_2 \in A_2)
\] 
for any sets $ A_1 \in E_1$, $ A_2 \in E_2$, where $ X_1: \Omega \to E_1$, and $ X_2: \Omega \to
E_2$.

We will show that they are not independent. Take $ A_1 = \{ 0 \} $ and $ A_2 = \{ 0 \} $. The
probability $ \P(X_1 \in A_1, X_2 \in A_2)$ is equal to

\begin{align*}
    \P(X_1 \in A_1, X_2 \in A_2) &= \P( X_1= 0, X_2 = 0)\\
				 &= \P( \1_{U \leq 1/2} = 0, \1_{U > 1/2} = 0)\\
				 &= \P(\omega > 1/2, \omega \leq 1/2)\\
				 &= \P(\emptyset)\\
				 &= 0.
\end{align*}

But we see that $ \P(X_1 \in A_1) = \P(\omega > 1/2)= 1/2$ and $ \P(X_2 \in A_2) = \P(\omega \leq
1/2) = 1/2$ and clearly $ 1/4 \neq 0$.
