
For the expectation value $ \E X$ to exist, we need

\[
    \sum_{x \in \N}^{} |x| \P_{X} (x) < \infty
.\] 

\begin{align*}
    \sum_{x\in \N}^{} |x| \frac{\lambda^{x} e^{-\lambda} }{x!} &= \sum_{x \in \N}^{} x \frac{\lambda^{x} e^{-\lambda} }{x!} \\
							       &= 0 +  \lambda \sum_{x\in \N_{\geq
								   1} }^{} x \lambda^{x-1}
							       \frac{e^{-\lambda}}{x!}\\
							       &= \lambda e^{-\lambda} \sum_{x\in \N_{\geq 1} }^{} \frac{d}{d\lambda} \frac{\lambda^{x} }{x!}  \\
							       &= \lambda e^{-\lambda}\frac{d}{d\lambda} e^{\lambda} \\
							       &= \lambda e^{-\lambda} e^{\lambda}\\
							       &= \lambda < \infty
\end{align*}
We find that the expectation value $ \E X$ exists and at the same time we find that $ \E X =
\lambda$.
