
The marginal distribution of $ X$ is

\begin{align*}
    \P(X = x) &= \P( \bigcup_{y \in Y} \{ X = x, Y = y \} )\\
	      &= \P( \bigcup_{y \in \{ 0,1 \} } \{ X = x, Y = y \})
\end{align*}

Assume $ x=0$

\begin{align*}
    \P(X=x) &= \P(X = 0, Y= 0) + \P(X=0, Y=1)\\
	    &= \alpha(1-\beta) + \alpha \beta\\
	    &= \alpha
\end{align*}

Assume $ x =1$

\begin{align*}
    \P(X=x) &= \P(X= 1, Y= 0) + \P(X=1, Y=1)\\
	    &= (1-\alpha)(1-\beta) + (1-\alpha)\beta\\
	    &= 1-\alpha
\end{align*}

Similarly, for the marginal distribution of $ Y$ we 
get

\begin{align*}
    \P(Y=y) &= 
    \begin{cases}
	1 -\beta & y = 0\\
	\beta & y = 1
    \end{cases}
\end{align*}
