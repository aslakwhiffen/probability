
The probability $ \P(X \geq x) $ is defined as 
\[
    \P(X \geq x) = \P( \{ \omega \in \Omega: X(\omega) \geq x \} )
.\] 
Since $ X$ is a random variable with a probability  
distribution $ \P_{X} $  that is continuous, we 
have that the distribution $ \P_{X} $  is defined on the intervals $ (a,b) \subset E = \R$
for which it holds that 

\[
    \P_{X}((a,b)) = \int_{a}^{b} f_{X} (x) dx \quad \mbox{by Theorem 2.16} 
.\] 

\begin{align*}
    \P_{X} (X \geq x) &= \int_{x}^{\infty} f_{X} (x) dx \\
		      &= \int_{x}^{\infty} \lambda e^{-\lambda u} du\\
		      &= \lambda \left(- \frac{1}{\lambda}  \right) \left[ e^{-\lambda u}
		  \right]_{x}^{\infty} \\
		      &= (-1) (0 - e^{\lambda x} ) = e^{-\lambda x} \quad \mbox{for} \, x > 0
\end{align*}
