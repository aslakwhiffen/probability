
By Theorem 2.16 (we let $ \Omega = \R$) if
\begin{enumerate}
    \item if $ f(x) \geq 0$
    \item $ \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f(x) dx = 1$
\end{enumerate}
then there is a unique probability distribution such that for all $ a, b \in \R$ with $ a < b$ we
have that $ \P((a,b)) = \int_{a}^{b} f(x) dx$. If we can show these two conditions hold for our $
f(x)$ then $ f$ is the density function of some distribution $ \P: 2^{\R} \to \R$. 

The first condition follows from the fact that $ -1 \leq \cos(x) \leq 1 $ so $ 0\leq 1+ \cos(x) \leq
2$, meaning that for $ x\in [0,\pi]$ the function $ f(x) = 1/\pi (1+ \cos(x)) \geq 0$ and otherwise
$ f(x) = 0 \geq 0$. 

To show the second condition, let's compute the integral 

\begin{align*}
    \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f(x) dx &= \int_{-\infty}^{0} f(x) dx + \int_{0}^{\pi} f(x) dx +
    \int_{\pi}^{\infty}f(x) dx \\
				    &= \int_{-\infty}^{0} (0) dx + \int_{0}^{\pi} \frac{1}{\pi}
				    (1+\cos(x)) dx + \int_{\pi}^{\infty}(0) dx\\
				    &= 0 + \frac{1}{\pi} \int_{0}^{\pi} (1+\cos(x)) dx + 0 \\
				    &= \frac{1}{\pi} \left[ x + \sin(x)  \right]_{0}^{\pi} \\
				    &= \frac{1}{\pi} \left[ (\pi-0) - (0-0)  \right] \\
				    &= 1
\end{align*}
