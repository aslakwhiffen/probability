

True. Let $ X_1: \Omega \to E_1$, $ X_2: \Omega \to E_2$, with $ E_1 \subset E_2$ and
$ \omega \in \Omega$, $ X_1 (\omega) = X_2 (\omega)$.
Take $ A_1 \subset E_1$

\[
    \P_{X_1} (A_1) = \P( \{ \omega \in \Omega: X_1(\omega) \in A_1 \} ) 
.\] 
Take $ A_2 \subset E_{2} $

\begin{align*}
    \P_{X_2} (A_2) &= \P ( \{ \omega \in \Omega: X_{2} (\omega)  \in A_2 \} ) \\
		   &= \P ( \{ \omega \in \Omega: X_1(\omega) \in A_2 \} )\\
		 &= \P_{X_1} (A_2)
\end{align*}
and so they have the same distribution.
